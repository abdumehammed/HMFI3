What are Compulsory Savings?

means mandatory deposits made for the purpose of enabling the individuals, groups or businesses making the saving to qualify to receive loans from an authorized institution and serve as collateral against the loans so obtained

Key Features of Compulsory Savings:

Compulsory savings, also known as mandatory or forced savings, refers to a type of savings program where individuals are required to regularly deposit a fixed amount of money into a savings account. This type of saving is often linked to microfinance institutions, credit unions, or loan agreements, where saving is a condition for accessing other financial services, such as loans. In the context of microfinance or cooperative models, compulsory savings can serve several purposes:

Loan Collateral: It may act as collateral or security for a loan.

Financial Discipline: It encourages saving habits among clients.

Risk Mitigation: It helps mitigate the risk for the institution providing loans by ensuring that the client has a financial stake. Typically, these savings cannot be withdrawn until certain conditions are met, such as loan repayment or the end of a specific contract.

What is Voluntary Saving?

Voluntary saving is a saving product that its amount and frequency of saving determined by the client itself. An individual client, member of the group, can have voluntary saving. A member of a group can withdraw its voluntary saving any time even when the group has loan outstanding with Harar MFI. Harar MFI pursues an open voluntary saving policy of accepting savings and deposit from borrowers; non-borrowers (both individuals and institutions). Voluntary saving products are passbook savings, box saving, mobile saving, fixed time deposits, and non-interest-bearing deposit services.

What is Regular Saving?

Harar MFI pursues an open voluntary saving policy of accepting savings and deposit from borrowers; non-borrowers (both individuals and institutions). Voluntary saving products are passbook savings, box saving, mobile saving, fixed time deposits, and non-interest-bearing deposit services.

What is Institutional Saving?

Institutional saving is product of a savings of Community Based Institutions (CBIs) such as Edir, Churches, Cooperatives, unions, different Associations, etc. CBIs can open institutional voluntary saving accounts and save their money with Harar MFI. In general, all saving products bear interest to their depositors and that Harar MFI pays 8% interest per annum on all saving products
