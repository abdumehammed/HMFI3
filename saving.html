What are Compulsory Savings?

Compulsory savings is a fixed amount that microfinance clients are required to save regularly before or while accessing financial services, particularly loans. This type of saving is enforced as part of the loan agreement, where clients need to deposit a certain percentage of their loan amount or a fixed amount into their savings account.

Key Features of Compulsory Savings:

Requirement for Loans: Many microfinance institutions require clients to have compulsory savings before they can access loans. This is to ensure the client has a financial buffer and demonstrates financial discipline. Loan Guarantee: The savings often act as collateral or a partial guarantee for the loan, providing the institution with a safety net in case of default. Encouraging Savings Behavior: By making savings mandatory, microfinance institutions aim to instill a savings culture among their clients, especially those who may not have a habit of saving regularly. Non-withdrawable: Clients are typically unable to withdraw these savings until they have repaid their loans or met specific conditions.
